# Thinker Notes

Use these as compact orientation notes, not exhaustive scholarship.

## Eastern thinkers

Laozi:

- Themes: Dao, non-coercive action, simplicity, softness, return, restraint.
- Useful contrast: Challenges excessive striving, control, and artificial order.

Zhuangzi:

- Themes: freedom, transformation, perspectival humility, play, release from rigid distinctions.
- Useful contrast: Loosens fixed identities and over-serious ambition.

Mencius:

- Themes: innate moral sprouts, benevolent governance, moral cultivation, humaneness.
- Useful contrast: Asks what nurtures or damages human goodness.

Confucius:

- Themes: ritual, role ethics, learning, humane relationships, self-cultivation through practice.
- Useful contrast: Emphasizes responsibility, tradition, and relational order.

Wang Yangming:

- Themes: unity of knowledge and action, innate knowing, moral practice in ordinary life.
- Useful contrast: Pushes insight to become lived action.

Zhang Zai:

- Themes: unity of all things, qi, moral responsibility, "forming one body with all things" as an interpretive spirit.
- Useful contrast: Expands the self beyond narrow private interest.

## Western thinkers

Aristotle:

- Themes: virtue, practical wisdom, flourishing, habit, moderation, telos.
- Useful contrast: Asks what kind of life and character an action forms.

Rousseau:

- Themes: natural freedom, inequality, social contract, corruption by social comparison.
- Useful contrast: Questions whether society makes people false to themselves.

Montesquieu:

- Themes: laws, institutions, separation of powers, moderation, political design.
- Useful contrast: Looks for structures that prevent domination.

Voltaire:

- Themes: reason, tolerance, anti-dogmatism, wit, criticism of fanaticism.
- Useful contrast: Uses sharp critique against pompous certainty.

Hegel:

- Themes: dialectic, recognition, history, contradiction, freedom becoming concrete.
- Useful contrast: Treats conflict as a path toward richer understanding.

Nietzsche:

- Themes: value creation, critique of herd morality, will to power, self-overcoming.
- Useful contrast: Challenges comfort, resentment, and inherited values.

Plato:

- Themes: forms, justice, education, soul, philosopher's ascent beyond appearances.
- Useful contrast: Asks what is truly good beyond opinion.

Socrates:

- Themes: questioning, examined life, humility before truth, ethical inquiry.
- Useful contrast: Refuses easy answers and exposes assumptions.

## Pairing hints

- Work pressure: Laozi vs Aristotle, Zhuangzi vs Nietzsche, Confucius vs Rousseau.
- Freedom: Zhuangzi vs Rousseau, Laozi vs Nietzsche, Wang Yangming vs Hegel.
- Leadership: Confucius vs Montesquieu, Mencius vs Aristotle, Zhang Zai vs Plato.
- AI and technology: Zhuangzi vs Hegel, Laozi vs Voltaire, Wang Yangming vs Aristotle.
- Anxiety and meaning: Laozi vs Nietzsche, Zhuangzi vs Socrates, Zhang Zai vs Rousseau.
